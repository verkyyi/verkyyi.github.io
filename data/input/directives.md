# Resume Directives

Global preferences for how resumes should be tailored. Write directives here manually, or let `/extract-directives` append them automatically from your edits to fitted files.

## Manual

### Keyword Matching

- Mirror exact terminology from the JD — if it says "cross-functional collaboration," use that phrase, not "worked with other teams"
- Include both acronym and spelled-out form on first use (e.g., "Natural Language Processing (NLP)")
- Use exact tool and technology names including version specifics when the JD does (e.g., "React.js" not just "React", "PostgreSQL" not "SQL databases")

### Summary

- Write a 2-3 sentence summary that mirrors the JD's top 3 requirements, includes the most impressive metric, and names the target role
- Front-load 3-5 of the most critical JD keywords into the summary
- Keep the summary under 50 words

### Bullet Writing

- Lead every bullet with a strong action verb — never "responsible for," "helped," "handled," or "supported"
- Follow the formula: Action Verb + Scope/Context + Quantified Result (e.g., "Reduced API latency by 40% across 12 microservices by implementing connection pooling")
- Use digits for all numbers ("12 engineers" not "twelve engineers")
- Include at least one quantified achievement per role — use metrics like revenue/cost impact ($), percentage changes (%), time saved, scale managed, or volume of output

### Section and Entry Ordering

- Reorder sections so the most JD-relevant section appears first after the summary
- Within each section, rank entries by relevance to the target role, not chronologically
- For the most recent 2-3 roles, show 4-6 bullets each; compress older roles to 1-2 bullets
- Front-load the top 3-5 bullets of each recent role to align with the JD's priorities

### Skills

- Order skills by relevance to the JD, with hard/technical skills first
- Every skill listed must be substantiated by at least one bullet in Experience or Projects
- Remove obsolete or assumed-basic skills (e.g., Microsoft Office, Git basics)

### What to Cut

- Remove self-assessed adjectives: "hard-working," "passionate," "results-driven," "team player" — show the trait through a quantified bullet instead
- Replace weak/vague language with specific claims backed by evidence
- Do not repeat a keyword more than 2-3 times across the entire resume — integrate in varied contexts

### Integrity

- Never claim skills or experience not present in the source resume
- When exact numbers are unavailable, keep the original wording rather than fabricating metrics
- Partial matches are fine — contextualize adjacent experience honestly rather than inflating it
